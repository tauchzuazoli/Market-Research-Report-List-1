<p><p>The nickel metal market includes two primary smelting types: nickeliferous limonite and garnierite. Nickeliferous limonite smelting involves extracting nickel from iron-rich lateritic ores, typically using high-pressure acid leaching for efficient recovery. In contrast, garnierite smelting focuses on processing the nickel-bearing mineral garnierite, often through conventional methods like rotary kilns and electric arc furnaces. Both methods cater to the growing demand for nickel in stainless steel production, batteries, and other industrial applications, influencing global supply dynamics.</p></p>
