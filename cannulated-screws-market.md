<p><p>The cannulated screws market is divided into two main types: headed cannulated screws and headless cannulated screws. Headed cannulated screws feature a prominent head designed for enhanced stability and alignment during orthopedic procedures, making them suitable for applications requiring secure fixation. In contrast, headless cannulated screws lack a traditional head, allowing for a more streamlined insertion and reducing soft tissue irritation. Both types are utilized in various surgical applications, particularly in the treatment of fractures and bone stabilization.</p></p>
